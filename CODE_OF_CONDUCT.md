# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**
- **Helping others learn and grow**
- **Sharing knowledge and resources**
- **Recognizing and celebrating contributions**

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual harassment, or bullying
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language, jokes, or imagery
- **Personal Attacks**: Personal insults, name-calling, or ad hominem attacks
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or off-topic messages
- **Spam**: Unsolicited commercial content or repetitive messages
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or harm
- **Disruption**: Disrupting discussions or events

## Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to:

- **Remove, edit, or reject** comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- **Temporarily or permanently ban** any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Communicate clearly** with the community about moderation decisions
- **Provide guidance** on how to participate constructively

### Community Members

Community members are responsible for:

- **Reading and understanding** this Code of Conduct
- **Following these guidelines** in all project spaces
- **Reporting inappropriate behavior** to maintainers
- **Helping create** a welcoming environment for all
- **Assuming good faith** in interactions with others

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include:

- **Using an official project email address**
- **Posting via an official social media account**
- **Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event**
- **Wearing project merchandise in public**

### Representation Guidelines

When representing BS9:

- **Be professional** and respectful in all interactions
- **Align with project values** of openness and inclusivity
- **Seek clarification** when unsure about official positions
- **Direct media inquiries** to project maintainers

## Enforcement

### Reporting Process

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

**Primary Contact:**
- Email: conduct@bs9.dev
- GitHub: [@xarhang](https://github.com/xarhang)
- Discord: Direct message to maintainers

**Alternative Contacts:**
- Any project maintainer
- GitHub Issues (for public concerns)
- Discord moderators

### What to Include in Reports

When reporting, please include:

- **Your contact information** (optional, but helpful for follow-up)
- **Description of the incident** (what happened, when, where)
- **Individuals involved** (if known and comfortable sharing)
- **Any evidence** (screenshots, links, messages)
- **Desired outcome** (what you would like to happen)

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: We'll acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We'll investigate the report thoroughly
3. **Action**: We'll take appropriate action based on findings
4. **Follow-up**: We'll follow up with the reporter on resolution
5. **Documentation**: We'll document the incident for future reference

### Possible Actions

Depending on the severity and nature of the incident, responses may include:

- **Warning**: Private warning about behavior
- **Education**: Request for education or training
- **Moderation**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Ban**: Permanent ban from project spaces
- **Legal**: Involvement of law enforcement if necessary

## Conflict Resolution

### Disagreements and Conflicts

We understand that disagreements happen. When conflicts arise:

1. **Assume good faith** - Most conflicts come from misunderstandings
2. **Communicate directly** - Try to resolve issues privately first
3. **Seek mediation** - Ask maintainers to help mediate if needed
4. **Focus on issues** - Address the problem, not the person
5. **Be open to feedback** - Be willing to learn and adjust

### Escalation Process

If direct resolution fails:

1. **Contact maintainers** - Reach out to project maintainers
2. **Formal mediation** - Request formal mediation process
3. **Community discussion** - If appropriate, involve broader community
4. **Final decision** - Maintainers make final determination

- **Diverse representation** in our community and leadership
- **Inclusive language** and communication
- **Accessible documentation** and tools
- **Welcoming environment** for all skill levels
- **Cultural sensitivity** in global interactions

- **Multi-language support** in documentation
- **Inclusive event planning** for meetups and conferences

## Consequences

### Short-term Consequences

- **Warning** for first-time minor violations
- **Temporary suspension** for repeated minor violations
- **Immediate removal** for serious violations

### Long-term Consequences

- **Permanent ban** for severe or repeated violations
- **Public acknowledgment** of serious violations
- **Legal action** for illegal activities
- **Industry notification** for severe cases
